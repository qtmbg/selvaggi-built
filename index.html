How We Work

Built for active healthcare environments.

Occupied healthcare renovation requires more than skilled construction. It requires planning around patient care, staff workflow, infection control, safety, access, utilities, inspections, and daily operations.

Selvaggi Built works inside hospitals, medical office buildings, surgery centers, behavioral health facilities, imaging suites, laboratories, and other clinical environments where disruption must be controlled and communication must be clear. Our process is designed to protect operations while moving the work forward with precision.

01

The process

Six steps
01

Listen on site.

We walk the facility before we quote, listening to the teams who know the space best. Every plan starts with a clear understanding of patient care, staff workflow, access, safety, infection control, and operational constraints.

02

Plan around operations.

The schedule serves the facility, not the build. We plan phasing, shutdowns, access, containment, deliveries, and work hours around active healthcare operations so construction moves forward with minimal disruption.

03

Coordinate the critical details.

Healthcare renovations depend on details that cannot be left to chance. We coordinate utility tie-ins, vendor access, equipment requirements, containment, inspections, logistics, and communication so field work stays aligned with facility needs.

04

Protect the environment.

Cleanliness, safety, infection control, and separation from active care areas are central to how we work. We plan and maintain construction zones with the discipline required for occupied healthcare settings.

05

Document everything.

We track daily progress, field conditions, safety controls, infection prevention measures, inspections, and closeout requirements so every stakeholder stays aligned from kickoff to turnover.

06

Finish ready for use.

Turnover is more than completion. We close out with the documentation, coordination, responsiveness, and craftsmanship required to help healthcare teams move into the finished space with confidence.

The Zero-Disruption Protocol

How we build
Active-facility renovation

A hospital is a living system. It cannot pause for construction. Our Zero-Disruption Protocol engineers the build sequence entirely around facility operations. Before tools are mobilized, we conduct ground-penetrating radar scans, map critical utilities, and establish isolation zones. ICRA Level 4 barriers, rigid wall systems, and HEPA-filtered negative air machines prevent cross-contamination. Every phase is reviewed and approved by facility directors and Inspectors of Record. Disruptive tie-ins are restricted to designated after-hours windows. We do not adapt our methods to the hospital. Our methods are built strictly for it.

I.

Pre-construction risk audit

Identify utility conflicts, assess infection control requirements, and lock phasing logistics before mobilization.

II.

ICRA barrier and containment plan

Rigid containment walls and continuous HEPA negative-air filtration installed before any work begins.

III.

Phased schedule with operations sign-off

All disruptive tie-ins, shutdowns, and utility work scheduled during pre-approved after-hours windows with facility sign-off.

IV.

Daily compliance log and weekly review

Continuous superintendent monitoring verifies containment holds.

Working with us

How do we start a project with you?

Start an RFP from any page — describe your project in a line or two and our scoping assistant structures it into a formal brief for our estimating team. If you would rather talk first, contact us directly; we respond within one business day.

Either way, we walk the facility before we quote. Every plan starts with a clear understanding of patient care, staff workflow, access, safety, and infection control on your site.

What areas do you serve?

California only. We are based in Anaheim and build across Southern California's healthcare facilities under CSLB License #1079406.

What types of facilities do you build in?

Hospitals, medical office buildings, surgery centers, behavioral health facilities, imaging and diagnostic suites, and clinical laboratories. See Expertise for how we approach each sector.

Do you take on small projects, or only major renovations?

Both. Recent work ranges from focused refurbishments — a nurses station, behavioral health dining rooms — to multi-phase renovation programs. What matters is not the size of the project; it is that the work happens inside an active healthcare environment, and that is what our process is built for.

02

Building in active facilities

Can you renovate while our facility stays open?

That is the core of what we do. Our Zero-Disruption Protocol engineers the build sequence around facility operations — phasing, containment, infection control, utility shutdowns, access, and daily documentation — so patient care continues while construction moves forward. Read more in How We Work.

How do you keep patients and staff safe during construction?

Containment is planned before tools are mobilized: ICRA barriers, rigid wall systems, HEPA-filtered negative air machines, sealed debris routes, and continuous superintendent monitoring to verify containment holds. Every phase is reviewed with facility directors and Inspectors of Record.

Will there be noise, shutdowns, or interruptions to care?

Disruptive tie-ins, shutdowns, and utility work are restricted to pre-approved after-hours windows with facility sign-off. The schedule serves the facility, not the build — work hours, deliveries, and access are planned around your operations.

03

Compliance and process

What ICRA level do you work under?

We regularly execute ICRA Level 3 and Level 4 containment. Not sure what your project requires? Our free ICRA Level Assessor generates the barrier tier, negative-pressure specification, and mitigation checklist to share with your infection control team.

Do you work on HCAI (formerly OSHPD) projects?

Yes — we plan and deliver work in HCAI-regulated facilities, coordinating phasing, inspections, and documentation with your Inspector of Record. Our compliance roadmap tool maps the permit path for your project type.

What happens after I submit an RFP?

Your brief goes straight to our estimating team and we respond within one business day. From there we schedule a facility walk — we listen on site before we quote, so the proposal reflects your real operational constraints, not assumptions.
